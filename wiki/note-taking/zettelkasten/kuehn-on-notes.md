Mannfred Kuehn's former blog "Taking Note" introduced me to Luhmann
and his Zettelkasten. Kuehn has taken down his blog (unfortunately),
but some of his blog posts are really insightful. Here are a few which
I want to draw attention to:

[Luhmann's Zettelkasten](https://web.archive.org/web/20201021184849/http://takingnotenow.blogspot.com/2007/12/luhmanns-zettelkasten.html)

["Loose Links" versus "Tight Links"](https://web.archive.org/web/20200510222450/http://takingnotenow.blogspot.com/2011/08/loose-links-versus-tight-links.html)

[Flat outlines versus hierarchical outlines](https://web.archive.org/web/20191108021402/http://takingnotenow.blogspot.com/2011/12/flat-outlines-versus-hierarchical.html)

[Zettelkasten and Bricolage](https://web.archive.org/web/20191020220521/https://takingnotenow.blogspot.com/2014/05/zettelkasten-and-bricolage.html)

[Different kinds of links](https://web.archive.org/web/20200225231711/http://takingnotenow.blogspot.com/2018/02/different-kinds-of-links.html)
since a train of thought has "implicit" links (what Kuehn calls "weiterführende Zettel")
and "branching" links (what Kuehn calls "ergänzende Zettel"). Kuehn
observes these are incidental to a paper-based Zettelkasten, not
essential to a Zettelkasten system "in general" (e.g., an electronic
Zettelkasten does not need to implement these).

Kuehn also wrote an essay [Some Idiosyncratic Reflections on Note-Taking in General and ConnectedText in Particular](https://web.archive.org/web/20100914031658/http://www.connectedtext.com/manfred.html)
worth reading.
